Final Reflections · Esame di Stato

Looking back, looking forward

A few honest thoughts at the end of my final year of high school: what I have learned, how I have grown, and where I want to go next.

01

The end of a chapter

Reaching the end of high school feels like closing a long and important chapter of my life. Over these years I have changed a great deal — not only in what I know, but in how I think, work and face new situations. The PCTO journey, from the third to the fourth year, played a central role in that growth.

Looking back, I realise that the most valuable lessons did not always come from a textbook. They came from stepping into real workplaces, meeting professionals, and discovering what is truly expected of me in the world beyond school. Each stage of the journey left me with something I will carry forward.

02

What I learned

Professional environment

I experienced real work settings, where I observed company operations, professional behaviour and the way teams cooperate every day. This exposure helped me understand what a real job actually demands — punctuality, reliability and respect for shared rules.

Communication & collaboration

During the internship and the company visits I improved my ability to communicate clearly and to work effectively within a group. I learned how to express my ideas respectfully, to listen to others, and to value points of view different from my own.

03

Personal growth

Responsibility

I became far more responsible with my time and tasks. Being punctual and organised gradually turned into second nature.

Initiative

I learned when to take initiative, ask the right questions and solve problems independently — key traits in any job.

Self‑confidence

With every new challenge I grew more confident in my abilities and more comfortable speaking up and sharing ideas.

04

Challenges & passion

The journey was not without its challenges. Entering unfamiliar environments, adapting to people I had never met and taking on tasks I had never done before pushed me out of my comfort zone. At first this felt intimidating, but every difficulty I worked through became a small victory and a reason to trust myself more.

Through it all, my passion for computer science never faded — it grew stronger. The Project Work on cybersecurity, in particular, reminded me why I love technology: the moment an abstract idea turns into something that genuinely works.

Overall satisfaction

This journey was not just about learning professional skills, but about shaping my mindset and discovering strengths I didn’t know I had.

Looking ahead

My next steps

This is only the beginning of a longer journey. I want to keep studying what I am most passionate about, and turn that passion into a profession in the world of technology and software development.

  • A Bachelor’s Degree in Computer Science (L‑31)
  • My preferred destination: the University of Milano‑Bicocca
  • TOLC‑S entrance test already completed
  • A long‑term career in software development and technology
Thank you

Thank you

I want to thank all the professionals, teachers, mentors and classmates who were part of this experience. Their guidance, patience and support were essential to my growth.

“This is only the beginning of a longer journey.”

GM Gabriel Mazzucchelli
